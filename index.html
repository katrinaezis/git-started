Totoro

Totoro is a giant fluffy bunny-like spirit. He is the spirit of the forest. He does not originate from any Japanese folk tale, but was imagined and created by Hayao Miyazaki, a Japanese film director, producter, screenwriter, animator, and manga artist. Totoro's name comes from a little girl, Mei, attempting to repeat the sound that he makes when she first met him. Totoro is able to fly, make trees and plants grow, and has other mysterious powers.

Story

My Neighbor Totoro is an animated film produced by Studio Ghibli, which is headed by Hayao Miyazaki. It follows two young girls, Satsuki and Mei, as they move with their father into an old house located closer to the hospital where their mother is recovering from a long-term illness. One day Mei sees two small, rabbit-like ears and follows the creature they belong to under the house. She discovers two sprits that lead her to a large camphor tree. As she follows them, she falls into a hidden hole at the base of the tree. There she finds Totoro, the king of the forest. Totoro accompanies Satsuki and Mei on many adventures while their mother is in the hospital.
